Virtual Onboarding

Written by Akshita Jain IIM BODHGAYA ADCOM Member

MBA is a very diverse and comprehensive program for professionals in all areas of business and commerce. The degree is meant to broaden students’ conceptual skills in critical thinking, leadership, communication, and problem-solving, while also allowing them to pursue a particular concentration or forge their own path as an entrepreneur. It is very essential for a successful manager to know about all the domains, at the same time should have in-depth knowledge of some specific one’s. All in all, MBA students are the live examples of the philosophy ‘Jack of all Trades, Master of One”.

In India IIMs are considered as the bench marker institutes for MBA. So, the question here is: How do IIM’s make their students Jack of all trades, master of One? Do IIM’s follow the system of major and minor? Or they have a completely different set of rules? What is their secret recipe?

The answer is NO, IIMs do not follow a systematic system of major and minor. This answer brings us to our next set of question, how then do they make their students fulfill the above-mentioned philosophy? And why do IIMs do not have this system of major and minor? The very simple and clear reason is that IIMs provide General Management degree to their students. They enable all of their students to be the ‘Jack of all Trades’. The students can work for a variety of companies and they are not domain specific or to phrase it properly not limited to only one domain. And if we see, Management is such a subject or a field where you should know everything related to the business. Your abilities should not be concise just for a particular area or thing in a business. This is the reason why IIMs follow this path.

Now, moving on to the 2nd part of the philosophy, If IIMs do not follow the system of major and minor, then how are they able to make the students ‘Master of One’. Here comes the concept of electives. For the second-year, students have to get themselves registered for elective courses which are the courses of their choice. They can choose any stream which they find interesting and most comfortable to build a career in. The elective courses help the students to gain deeper knowledge and understanding in the field of their choice along with enabling the students to acquire the skillset required by the corporate world in that field. IIMs follow this rule of electives so that all the opportunities are available for every single student and as mentioned above no student should find himself or herself less capable than any other person to sit for a particular company or in the corporate world in general.

Here, in IIM Bodh Gaya, we have domain specific electives and courses that are floated for all the students. As early as 3rd term the discussions and the presentations by various faculty members starts happening and after all the students get some basic knowledge regarding the courses of their interest, they are guided through an entire comprehensive process of how to choose their electives and what combination of subjects will work the best for them and the industry requirements.

After this introduction phase, the students must get themselves registered for their preferred courses. They have to select the electives/courses of their choice for their entire second year till the end of term 3. However, just to make things clear, not all IIMs make the students select the courses in one go. Some IIMs let the students choose their electives at the start of their 4th, 5th, and 6th terms, i.e., the students select at the starting of every term what set of subjects they want to study in that particular term. But in general, the structure/system of electives is same in all the IIMs. A very interesting and good thing we have in IIM Bodh Gaya is, if some students feel like a particular subject became very important according to the industry standards and they want the subject to be introduced in the course portfolio, they can approach the Academic Committee and put forth this point in front of them. After this the Academic Committee take this forward on behalf of them to the _. Then there will be further processes including voting on that particular subject as in after everyone is done with selecting their electives there will be final counting. In that counting if 20% (this is not the final percentage, it can vary as per the rules and the criteria set by the PGP Committee (faculty members), all decisions regarding this are solely as per their discretion) or more students of the batch opted for that subject/course then that subject/course will get introduced. The structure of IIMs is such that all students should have the basic knowledge of all the domains and must excel in at least one of them. This is the reason we don’t have a system/concept of major and minor. Still, this is understood by all of us that if we have a minimum 60% of our chosen subjects from a particular domain that becomes our major and a minimum of 30% subjects from a particular domain make that domain our minor (we can have multiple minor domains).
