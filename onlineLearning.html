Online, On line?

Written by Shreya Doshi IIM BODHGAYA ADCOM Member

MBA is a very diverse and comprehensive program for professionals in all areas of business and commerce. The degree is meant to broaden students’ conceptual skills in critical thinking, leadership, communication, and problem-solving, while also allowing them to pursue a particular concentration or forge their own path as an entrepreneur. It is very essential for a successful manager to know about all the domains, at the same time should have in-depth knowledge of some specific one’s. All in all, MBA students are the live examples of the philosophy ‘Jack of all Trades, Master of One”.

Am I audible? Am I visible? these lines have become the daily chant of the students nowadays, courtesy: the COVID pandemic. No, this is not another blog discussing the pandemic and its ill-effects, just an experience of a student facing such situation for the first time.

Being totally new to the concept, I was both nervous and excited at the same time as to how things will unfold as I was prepping for the two-year MBA program at IIM Bodh Gaya. As the program commenced, preparatory classes were provided so that we can get accustomed to the online platform. Seeing my batchmates fit into small virtual windows instead of a class was a new experience altogether. This was my very first time being taught online but it didn’t seem as troublesome as I thought it to be. I had many apprehensions as to how the professors will be teaching in the new medium and how will the learning take place. But all my misconceptions got cleared, the faculty here being extremely supportive, helped us avert all the roadblocks. They constantly interacted with us to ensure that we get deeper understanding of the concept. I completely acknowledge the fact that it is not easy to teach in the online mode, but our professors definitely made it better for us.

The learning process consists of numerous case study discussions, group projects and presentations, these could become challenging tasks when you work with people you have not ever contacted personally. I agree this was no smooth ride, but definitely a fun one. The late-night meets discussing various concepts and learning new concepts is what I think we all will cherish. Being a fresher, working in teams with my experienced peers added a lot to my learning. Prior to this, I was quite apprehensive of the concept of befriending people online but it didn’t turn out as I thought it to be, instead I ended up connecting with people who I have not even met or seen.

Time flew by quickly while meeting the deadlines, taking quizzes, enjoying various events with my batchmates and numerous insightful webinars, each experience reassuring me of my decision of taking up an MBA program. Now, here I am, gearing up for the second term with same enthusiasm and energy.

There is no denying the fact that online learning is not a cakewalk and is different from the offline mode in terms of experience offered. However, making most of the situation is what managers are supposed to do. Uncertainties and challenges are part and parcel of a manager’s life and so, instead of being apprehensive and complaining, I have tried my best to accept and give my best in this situation.

Nobody knows what the future holds and when we’ll come back to the normal, but one thing is sure that being optimistic will definitely alleviate the anxiety for the same.
